Sweet Granadilla

A delicious fruit from Latin America

Sweet granadilla, also known as passiflora ligularis grows mainly in Peru but also as north as Mexico and south as northern Argentina. It grows in tropical climates ranging from 59°F to 64°F. The plant consists of abundant leaves and greenish-white flowers. The fruit consists a orange-yellow shell on the oustide and dark seeds surrounded by transluscent, gelatinous pulp on the inside. The fruit is fragrant and has a soft sweet taste.
